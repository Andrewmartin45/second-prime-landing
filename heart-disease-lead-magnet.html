The 10 Blood Markers Your Doctor Isn't Running
A complete reference guide to the cardiovascular biomarkers that matter, what they mean, and what to do about them.

Standard blood panels catch disease. They are not designed to catch risk. The difference between those two things is often a decade of silent damage to your arteries, your heart, and your metabolic health.

Fifty percent of heart attack victims had normal LDL cholesterol. That number exists because conventional medicine is still testing the wrong things, or interpreting the right things against the wrong standards.

This guide covers 10 biomarkers that give a more complete picture of cardiovascular risk. For each one, you will find what the marker does, why it matters, what optimal actually looks like, and what interventions are available across medication, supplements, peptides, and lifestyle.

This is not a treatment protocol. It is a starting point for a more informed conversation about your biology.

A NOTE FROM THE FOUNDER
Andrew Martin
FOUNDER, SECOND PRIME

Ten years ago I was training people in person. Coaching nutrition, building supplement protocols, running sessions. I had a client who just wasn't showing up for himself. He missed sessions, skipped the nutrition work, left his supplements on the shelf. I let him off the hook. I didn't push hard enough, and if I'm being honest, I didn't have the right tools to help him the way he actually needed.

A few months after he cancelled, he died of a heart attack at 52. He had two young kids.

That hit me in a way I still carry. I felt like I let him down. Like I let his family down. I lost a lot of sleep over it, and I made a decision: I was going to learn everything I possibly could about what the data actually tells us about cardiovascular function, and I was going to build the tools I wished I had back then.

That work led me here. Ten years of going deep into functional medicine, cardiovascular biology, and the gap between what conventional medicine measures and what the science actually shows. This guide is the result of that. The 10 biomarkers I would have wanted that client to know about. The interventions that exist. The standards that actually matter.

Conventional medicine does a good job of catching disease. It is not designed to catch risk. That distinction is what this document is built around.
