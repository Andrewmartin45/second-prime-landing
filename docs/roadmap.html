Tests 05 – 09

Test 05

Vibrant Blood Panel

Micronutrients · Minerals · Vitamins · Inflammatory Markers · Cardiometabolic Health · Hormones · Immunity · Thyroid · Electrolytes

Blood Draw

180+ biomarkers assessed across every major system. This is the diagnostic foundation of your protocol — identifying root causes before prescribing solutions.

Preparation

  • Schedule the draw between 6–9 AM. Fast for 12 hours prior. Water is permitted.
  • Menstruating women: schedule between days 19–22 of your cycle (or day 3 if that window is far away). Record the day of your cycle.
  • Discontinue non-medically necessary supplements 7 days before the draw.
  • No hard lower-body workouts within 3 days. No any workouts within 24 hours of the draw.

Kit & Collection

  • A kit ships directly to your home with instructions. Set up the Vibrant app to book a draw.
  • Mobile phlebotomist: ~$60–$100. Comes to your location and handles FedEx shipping.
  • Local lab: You handle FedEx shipping after the draw. Any local lab capable of drawing for a Vibrant kit will work — call ahead to confirm.
Test 06

Gut Zoomer

Gut Microbiome · Pathogenic Bacteria · Yeast · Viruses · Parasites · GI Inflammation

Stool

70% of the immune system resides in the gut. This panel maps your entire microbiome and screens for pathogens, parasites, leaky gut, inflammation, and maldigestion. An imbalanced gut is a root cause of hormonal disruption, metabolic dysfunction, and autoimmune disease.

Restrictions Before Collection

  • No antibiotics or probiotics for 14 days prior.
  • Avoid laxatives, pepto bismol, antacids, glutamine, or apple cider vinegar for 5 days prior.
  • Performance supplements (creatine, whey) are permitted.

Collection & Shipping

  • Read instructions fully before starting. Prepare your collection area in advance.
  • Ship to FedEx immediately after collection — or call for a free pickup.
  • If shipping the same day isn't possible, store in the refrigerator. Ship the next available day. Do not store longer than 3 days.
Test 07

Food Sensitivity Complete

209 Foods · Preservatives · Artificial Sweeteners · Emulsifiers · Food Dyes · Pesticides

Blood Draw

No action required from you. This panel is run directly from your blood draw (Test 5). No additional collection, kit, or scheduling is needed on your end.

Identifies inflammatory dietary triggers across 209 common Western foods and food additives. Chronic food inflammation is linked to heart disease, diabetes, arthritis, and autoimmune conditions. Removing these triggers is one of the fastest ways to reduce systemic inflammation.

What It Tests

  • 209 common Western foods
  • Preservatives & artificial sweeteners
  • Emulsifiers & food dyes
  • Pesticide residues

Why It Matters

  • Identifies hidden dietary inflammation
  • Linked to heart disease, diabetes, arthritis
  • One of the fastest levers for reducing systemic inflammation
  • Informs your personalized nutrition protocol
How This Works
  • Your blood draw sample (Test 5) is automatically used for this panel — no separate collection required.
  • Results are returned alongside your full blood panel and interpreted as part of your protocol.
  • Elimination and reintroduction strategies will be built directly into your nutrition plan.
Test 08

Hormone Zoomer

Estrogen · Progesterone · Testosterone / DHEA-S · Cortisol · Adrenal Rhythm

Saliva + Urine

Assesses how your hormones fluctuate over a full 24-hour period — a level of insight not available from standard blood serum tests. Hormonal imbalances at this level affect sleep, mood, body composition, muscle mass, cognition, and energy.

Before Each Saliva Collection — Avoid for 30 Minutes

  • Mouthwash, brushing teeth, chewing gum, smoking, eating, or drinking.

Day of Collection — Avoid Entirely

  • Caffeine, alcohol, flossing, and intense exercise. Light walking is permitted.

Urine Collection Schedule

  • Morning upon waking (7–8 AM)
  • Before lunch (11:30 AM – 12:30 PM)
  • Before dinner (4:30–5:30 PM)
  • Before bed (~9 PM)

Saliva Collection Schedule

  • 30 min after waking
  • 60 min after waking
  • Evening, before dinner
  • At night, before bed

Menstruating women: Collect on day 19, 20, or 21 of your cycle (if regular); 5 days before expected menstruation (if irregular); any non-bleeding day (if unpredictable).

Test 09

Toxic Heavy Metals

20 Metals & Elements Including Arsenic · Lead · Mercury

Urine

Screens for 20 metals and elements. Heavy metal toxicity is an under-recognized root cause of neurological disorders, GI dysfunction, autoimmune disease, and elevated oxidative stress. The WHO lists 13 heavy metals as significant public health threats.

Identifying your exposure levels allows us to design targeted detoxification strategies within your protocol.

Collection

  • No fasting or special preparation required.
  • Collect urine in the provided cup. Transfer to the tube up to the 15ml line. Label accurately.
  • Handle carefully. Do not store in the refrigerator — ship immediately.

Shipping

  • Ship via FedEx immediately after collection. Samples held longer than 12 hours may be rejected.

Body Scan

We assess not just weight, but the quality of your weight — how muscle and fat are distributed. Body composition data directly informs nutrition, training, and supplementation decisions.

A precise snapshot of your nutritional reality. For 7 days, you document food intake, digestive symptoms, meal timing, and associated energy and mood patterns. This uncovers deficiencies and inflammatory patterns driving your symptoms.

What You'll Track

Test 13

CO2 Tolerance

Nervous System Stress Response · Breathing Efficiency · Autonomic Regulation

Breathing

Breathing is a direct window into your nervous system. The CO2 Tolerance Test measures how long you can exhale before the drive to inhale returns — a reliable proxy for how your body manages stress at the physiological level.

When stress rises, CO2 accumulates. Your tolerance score tells us how efficiently your nervous system is regulating that response. This informs a targeted breathwork protocol built around your specific baseline.

How to Perform the Test

  1. Sit or lie down comfortably.
  2. Take three normal nasal inhale breaths.
  3. Take one full nasal inhale breath.
  4. Start your timer and exhale as slowly as possible for as long as you can.
  5. Stop the timer when you pause the exhale, swallow, panic, or run out of air.
  6. Record your score in the "Visible Stressor Assessment: [YOUR NAME]" Google Sheet.

A short video walkthrough is available in your client portal to demonstrate proper form before you attempt the test.

Test 14

Survival Strength

Grip Strength · Single-Leg Strength · Longevity Indicators · Physical Preparedness

Fitness

Grip strength is a more reliable predictor of longevity than blood pressure — it is a proxy for total systemic strength. Single-leg strength reveals power, balance, muscle mass, and fall risk. These two metrics tell us more about your long-term physical trajectory than most lab panels.

Grip Strength Test — Max Effort Dead Hang

  1. Find a pull-up bar or similar overhead structure.
  2. Perform a maximum-duration hang.
  3. Record your time in the Google Sheet.

Leg Strength Test — 8RM Rear Foot Elevated Split Squat

  1. Watch the instructional video in your client portal before attempting.
  2. Gradually build to the heaviest weight you can complete for 8 reps on each leg.
  3. Record the dumbbell weight per hand and your bodyweight in the Google Sheet. (e.g. if you use a 20 lb dumbbell in each hand, record 20 lbs.)

If unable to perform with weight, use bodyweight. If that is not possible, record "N/A" — this is still useful data.

Test 15

VO2 Max

Cardiovascular Endurance · Oxygen Utilization · Mortality Risk Indicator

Fitness

VO2 max is the strongest single predictor of mortality we have. A low VO2 max relative to an elite score corresponds to a 500% increased mortality risk over the next decade — higher than smoking (41%), coronary artery disease (29%), diabetes (40%), or high blood pressure (21%).

This number tells us your cardiovascular ceiling and informs how aggressively we can train your system.

Option 1 — 1.5 Mile Run Test (Recommended)

  1. Warm up moderately.
  2. Run 1.5 miles as fast as possible on flat ground. On a standard track: 6 laps.
  3. Record your time in the Google Sheet.

Option 2 — 1 Mile Walk Test (For those with limitations)

  1. Find a flat 1-mile route.
  2. Warm up with 3 minutes of walking.
  3. Walk 1 mile as quickly as possible without jogging. Time it.
  4. Take your heart rate 10 seconds after finishing.
  5. Record time and heart rate in the Google Sheet.

Complete one test only. Choose based on your current capacity.

Movement Screen

Range of Motion · Flexibility · Mobility · Joint Function

Zoom Meeting

Full joint range of motion is foundational to physical performance and injury prevention. Restricted mobility creates compensatory movement patterns that increase wear on joints and elevate injury risk. This screen identifies those restrictions before they compound.

Results drive targeted mobility work that is integrated into your training and recovery protocols.

This is performed live with a member of our team — not a self-assessment. Schedule your appointment as early as possible to avoid delaying the timeline.
